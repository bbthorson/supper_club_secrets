## Tier 1 — Critical (fix before locking the book)

### 1.1 🔴 Elijah's apartment contradicts its own locked series seed (Ch17)
- **Where:** `m3_17_sharpening_the_knives.md`, the Oliver/Elijah press-kit scene — "They'd
  agreed to work at Elijah's apartment because it was closer to midtown and Elijah had a
  printer... Oliver had spread his research across the dining table..."
- **Problem:** `canon library/locations/elijahs-apartment.md` states flatly: *"Elijah's
  Apartment... appears only in Ch8... The group has never been inside. Elijah has never
  hosted, and his home staying unknown to them is a deliberate seed for the Book 6 hosting
  payoff."* `continuity.md` and `series_plan.md` both carry this as a locked cross-book
  thread. Ch17 has Oliver spending an extended work session inside the apartment, directly
  falsifying "never been inside."
- **Fix (prose, recommended):** Relocate the Oliver/Elijah press-kit session to a neutral
  location — Emma's apartment, Elijah's office, a coffee shop — preserving the Book 6 payoff
  intact. **Alternative (author call):** if the scene should stay, the Book 6 seed language
  needs narrowing (e.g., "the group collectively has never gathered there" rather than "no
  one has ever been inside," since Oliver alone visiting once for a work session is a
  materially smaller reveal than the group discovering it together). This is a load-bearing
  series seed, so treat it as an exception to the normal "prose wins" default — confirm which
  side should move before fixing.

## Tier 2 — High priority (before manuscript sign-off)

### 2.1 🟡 POV head-hops in Meal 3 that the 2026-07-11 sweep didn't reach
The prior audit's Tier 3.1 POV sweep covered Meals 1–2 only. This pass found the same class
of violation, uncaught, in Meal 3 — all three chapters are `pov: Emma`, but each asserts
another character's interior as narrated fact rather than Emma's observation:
- `m3_15_a_bitter_taste.md` — "for Noah, who believed every system was legible if you
  understood its inputs, the not-knowing was its own kind of pain."
- `m3_16_the_recipe_for_revenge.md` — "He was in systems mode now, seeing the problem as an
  engineering challenge rather than a betrayal." (Noah's interior asserted as fact)
- `m3_16_the_recipe_for_revenge.md` — "Jasper, who had been watching the scene unfold with a
  mix of awe and shame—his friends building something remarkable out of the wreckage he'd
  created—finally spoke up."
- **Fix (prose):** Small-radius reframes to Emma-observable ("seemed," "looked like"),
  matching the treatment already applied to the Meal 1–2 passages in the 2026-07-11 fix.

### 2.2 🟡 POV drift into Oliver's interior (Ch24)
- **Where:** `m4_24_the_proof.md:71` — "'She used our words,' Oliver said, and there was
  wonder in it — a man who'd spent years writing careful arguments in corners of the internet
  where no one knew his name, watching someone with real reach fire them at the world."
- **Problem:** Ch24 is `pov: Emma`. This line narrates Oliver's history as omniscient fact
  rather than something Emma could observe. Note: this does *not* over-expose the Book 2
  secret — Oliver already told the group about his moderator background on-page in Ch15 (per
  `subplot_threads.md`'s own note, "the Ch15 parenthetical names the role, not the
  communities or handle"), so what's still held back for Book 2 is the identity/handle, not
  the general fact. The issue is purely the head-hop.
- **Fix (prose):** Reframe to something Emma could actually perceive — e.g., "there was
  something in his voice she'd never heard before, a private satisfaction she didn't have the
  context for."

### 2.3 🟡 Timeline wording slip — a group text can't arrive with a one-day lag
- **Where:** `m2_09_a_pinch_too_much.md`, opening lines — "Then, Thursday evening, his phone
  buzzed with Elijah's message." vs. `m2_08_the_bottom_line.md:67–73`, where Elijah composes
  and posts that exact group-chat message on **Wednesday evening**.
- **Problem:** "Buzzed with" reads as real-time receipt, not Noah opening a notification a
  day late. The date itself is fine (`timeline_ledger.md`'s Oct 8/Ch9 row has Noah reacting
  Thursday), but the verb implies the message physically arrived Thursday, which contradicts
  Ch8's Wednesday post.
- **Fix (prose):** Reword to something like "he finally opened Elijah's message" or add a
  clause noting he'd silenced the group chat Wednesday night and only saw it Thursday.

### 2.4 🟡 `00_story_outline.md` overstates the Ch12 reveal vs. the shipped chapter
- **Where:** `00_story_outline.md`'s Meal 2 bullet ("Jasper's reveal: Brenda confirmed the
  LLC belongs to the restaurant mogul buying up Williamsburg") and its Mystery Solution Grid
  row ("How was it discovered? ... Brenda (via Jasper) confirmed the mogul's identity").
- **Problem:** In the shipped Ch12, Brenda never names Pike or confirms his identity — she
  only reacts with fear and refuses to say more; Elijah explicitly says, "We just don't have
  a face yet." Identity is deferred to Ch13 (Pike introduces himself) and confirmed via
  Noah's press-release match in Ch14. Ch12's own frontmatter `audit_notes` already document
  this deferral — the outline document just wasn't updated after the revision.
- **Fix (tracking):** Update the outline's Ch12 bullet and Mystery Solution Grid to match the
  shipped version: Brenda's reaction confirms scale/danger, not identity.

### 2.5 🟡 `00_story_outline.md` Timeline table is stale against Ch13's frontmatter
- **Where:** `00_story_outline.md` Timeline table, row "Week 3, Tues/Wed | Ch13–16."
- **Problem:** Ch13's own frontmatter spans **Monday→Wednesday** (Oct 12–14) — its
  `audit_notes` say the Monday beat was added specifically to resolve an earlier day-jump
  compression and fill a Tuesday void (the 2026-07-11 fix). The outline's top-level table
  never picked up that change.
- **Fix (tracking):** Change the row to "Week 3, Mon–Wed" to match Ch13's frontmatter.

## Tier 3 — Craft (real, but subjective — author's call on how much to chase)

### 3.1 🟡 Negative parallelism ("Not X — it's Y") remains a book-wide habit, reduced but not gone
The five author edits before this audit removed roughly a fifth of the book's instances
(mechanical count: 32 → 27 book-wide). The device still recurs densely enough to read as a
reflex in three places:
- `m1_01_the_missing_hot_sauce.md` — 4 instances in one chapter (lines 55, 65, 103, 141 —
  confirmed independently by both the prose-craft refresh and the Meal 1 continuity pass).
- Meal 3 (Ch13, 15, 16, 17) — 5–6 instances spread across the meal, not clustered in one
  scene, but a consistent per-chapter tic (confirmed independently by the Meal 3 continuity
  pass's light AI-tells glance).
- `m4_24_the_proof.md` / `m4_20_ghost_kitchen.md` boundary — 2–3 instances each, both climax
  chapters.
- **Suggested fix:** Author's call on how much more to trim. The worst single-sentence
  offenders (`m1_01` especially) are the highest-value targets if more trimming is wanted; the
  device is genre-legitimate in small doses and doesn't need to hit zero.

### 3.2 🔵 Recycled physical tell — Elijah's jaw, three variations in one chapter
`m3_14_the_seamless_supper.md` uses jaw-tension as Elijah's default anger marker three times
in the same chapter: line 98 ("his jaw was clenched"), line 130 ("His jaw worked"), line 158
("his jaw was tight"). Not a catalogued voice tic (his are checking his phone and going
cold/clinical) — worth varying two of the three.

### 3.3 🔵 Recycled phrase — "the version of himself [in the forums]"
Near-identical construction used twice for Oliver — `m3_15_a_bitter_taste.md:112` ("the
version of himself that existed in forums and private communities") and
`m4_24_the_proof.md:51` ("the version of himself that lived in the forums") — describing the
same characterization beat at the book's two biggest Oliver moments, plus a third relative
use for Jasper (`m4_22_the_dive.md:43`, "the version of himself he performed at dinner
parties"). Since `m3_15` lands first and is better-earned, the `m4_24` instance is the one
worth rewriting.

### 3.4 🔵 Magic-adverb clustering — "quietly," 20 uses book-wide, 7 in the finale
About half are Oliver's catalogued quiet register (allowlisted, out of scope for this pass).
The rest attach to characters/narration with no catalogued basis — Olivia ("genuinely,
quietly brilliant," `m2_07:62`), Noah (`m3_15:130`, `m4_25:161`), Elijah ("very quietly,"
`m3_17:112`), Emma (`m4_25:227`), and plain narration ("quietly pulled," "folded... Quietly").
The word is doing generic "this moment matters" work rather than characterizing. Lower
priority than 3.1–3.3 since none of it is clustered as tightly.

### 3.5 🔵 Chapter-ending "epiphany button" — 7 of 9 flagged instances remain
Two of the nine chapter-closers flagged in the prior prose-craft pass were fixed by the
recent edits (`m2_09`'s "Maybe there was more to this than he'd thought" and `m3_16`'s
standalone "Grateful." are both now folded into flowing sentences). The remaining seven
(`m1_05`, `m2_06`, `m2_12`, `m4_20`, `m4_21`, `m4_23`, `m4_24`) still close on a short,
standalone declarative/fragment sentence. Author's call on whether to vary more of these —
the two that were fixed read noticeably better integrated.

### 3.6 🔵 "Shoulders dropped" relaxation beat repeats within one chapter
`m1_01_the_missing_hot_sauce.md:81` ("felt her shoulders drop half an inch") and `:141`
("Emma's shoulders dropped an inch. Then another.") — same tell twice in one chapter. Minor.

### 3.7 🟡 Red-herring layer is thin relative to `ai_instructions.md` §6
The Ch4 theory competition (kidnapping, Big Hot Sauce, socialite, aliens/CIA, pepper-market
destabilization) is comedy, dismissed within the same chapter, never seriously investigated
or eliminated by evidence — the Meal 2 investigation goes straight to the correct LLC/patent
thread with no false trail costing real page-time. The mystery is very fair-play-solid on the
"solvable by Meal 3" axis but thin on "2–3 legitimate red herrings." Not fixable in the
current manuscript without redrafting investigation chapters — **flagged for future books**:
give at least one Meal 2 lead a plausible wrong turn that gets investigated and cleanly
eliminated, rather than resolving every thread as immediately correct.

### 3.8 🔵 Olivia's on-page micro-arc is the thinnest of the six
Consistent characterization and one real crack of vulnerability (Ch20's fear spiral) plus the
Ch25 button (proposing the club's future), but her arc runs mostly in service of others
(comforting Emma, managing Oliver, running the network) rather than her own throughline. This
is by design — `series_plan.md` defers her real arc to Book 4 — but she's the least visibly
changed of the six by book's end. No fix needed for Book 1; worth remembering the Book 4
payoff needs to land harder given how little runway she got here.

### 3.9 🔵 One line brushes the tone ceiling without ever cashing it in
`m3_13_service_for_one.md`, Pike's dialogue: "If not me, then someone with a lot less
interest in being gentle about it." Rhetorically implies a worse, possibly violent actor
waiting in the wings — thriller-adjacent phrasing against §7's "never exceed 7" rule. Never
developed or paid off, so it stays a flourish rather than an actual escalation, but it's the
single riskiest line in the book against the ceiling. Author's call: soften to something like
"someone with a lot less patience for the process," or leave as-is since it's never realized
on-page.

### 3.10 🔵 Jasper's spice-named accounts slightly pre-empt Ch18's "food metaphor" surprise
Noah names his anonymous email accounts "Cumin, Cardamom, Saffron, Sumac... because Emma
would appreciate that" in `m3_17`, itself a food-flavored gesture from Noah. Ch18 then plays

contradiction, just a slightly softened novelty.

### 3.11 🔵 Hank's "three weeks" is a mild underestimate
`m4_25_family_meal.md:183` — "The bottle I'd given Dorothy three weeks before." Per
`timeline_ledger.md` and the Sep 27/30 dates for Hank's last appearance/stall surrender, Ch25

### 3.12 🔵 Emma's Ch14 "since spring" doesn't map to a dated Book 1 event
`m3_14_the_seamless_supper.md` — "the thing she'd been carrying alone since spring." Emma quit
Bistro Lavande roughly mid-September (three weeks before the Oct 4 opening), so "since spring"
doesn't anchor to anything on the books. Plausibly intentional (a longer precarity undercurrent

### 3.13 🔵 The Gilded Fern "Tuesday morning" visit brushes its stated hours
`m2_07_gathering_the_ingredients.md:34` — "Tuesday morning, she stood in The Gilded Fern."
`the-gilded-fern.md`'s operating rule is "Open daily, 11am–7pm." Not an outright violation if
read as late morning, but worth tightening to "late morning" or "just past eleven" if the
author wants to close the gap.

### 3.14 🔵 `m2_10_loose_lips.md` frontmatter doesn't note its legitimate feed excursion
The chapter's frontmatter lists `pov: "Jasper"` only, but the closing scene (after the `---`
break) is entirely Brenda's interiority. This is a legitimate feed excursion under §5
("limited excursions into other feeds are allowed... dramatic irony is intentional"), not a

### 3.15 🔵 Heading-level inconsistency between meal openers
`m1_01_the_missing_hot_sauce.md` opens its meal section at `# Meal One` (H1); the equivalent
header in `m2_06_a_fine_sieve.md` is `## Meal Two` (H2). Structurally identical headers using
different levels — cosmetic, but §10 calls for consistent heading conventions. Likely just
needs `m1_01`'s header bumped to `##`.
