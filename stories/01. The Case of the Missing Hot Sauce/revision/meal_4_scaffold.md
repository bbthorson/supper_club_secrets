## What Meal 4 has to accomplish
1. **Resolve the case** — the swarm campaign turns public sentiment, Pike's project collapses, Hank comes home.
2. **Land the thesis.** The swarm is **Emma's worldview enacted**: given the chance, a hundred small voices choose the guy with the story and the heritage peppers. This is the direct refutation of Pike's "it was inevitable" — it was never inevitable; people choose the little guy when they get to.
3. **Pay off the characters** (see [Character landings](#character-landings)).
4. **Stay cozy.** Tone never exceeds 7. The victory is collective and quiet, an exhale, not a fist-pump.

## How Pike loses (and doesn't)
- **Off-page and silent.** Per his profile, Pike has *no playbook for a hundred small voices that owe him nothing.* He goes quiet, calls lawyers/PR/accountant, tries to *manage* a thing that can't be managed. We see him only through the campaign's surface — a terse PR non-statement, an investor pulling out. **No second on-page Pike scene** (Ch13 was his one appearance; his absence here is the point).
- **Not destroyed — regroups.** Investors pull back, the waterfront project is shelved, the C&D is withdrawn, the patent challenged. He still has three restaurants and lawyers on retainer. Recurring-villain seed intact.
- **Closure via headlines.** Pike's collapse reaches the reader the way it reaches the group — links and headlines dropped in the group chat (an investor pulls out, the project is shelved, the C&D is withdrawn). We never cut to him on-page.
- **The squeeze reverses — threaded.** Across the breakthrough and the dinner (not one beat), the pressure on Emma quietly lifts: an editor circles back, a client rebooks. A threaded payoff of the Meal 3 stakes.

### Road-log conceit: Diners, Drive-Ins & Dives
Each road log is datelined at a roadside food spot — a diner, a drive-in, a dive — where Jasper stops to work the phones, catch a lead, or hit bottom. The recurring venue type keeps the logs food-flavored, gives each a vivid micro-setting and a local face, and winks at the show. Three logs, one of each venue, span the search days; the find ("Farm to Table") is the full chapter.

## Character landings

- **Emma (focal).** From hiding her precarity to letting the group in (Olivia confide paid off) — and her instincts vindicated: "Big Hot Sauce" was real, and people *do* choose the little guy. She **drives** the resolution: her narrative is the campaign's heart, and weaponizing industry grudges is her play. The squeeze lifting is her concrete relief. Secret stays opaque. - **Noah.** Tech served the cause; the human/analog lesson lands. **Reconciles with Jasper** — the apology is his real arc beat. The Noah-conduit (being the one Jasper reports to from PA) is the bridge.
- **Olivia.** Her network is the distribution engine — her social gift weaponized for good. She's the one who already knew Emma's fear (the confide); fierce-protector arc resolves.
- **Jasper.** Redemption by finding Hank; chaos finally pointed right. Learns that strategic quiet can be more useful than noise. The Chelsea/class sting from Ch15 is absorbed, not resolved (seed).
- **Hank.** Returns from Meadowlight; files a proper trademark (pro bono); the group helps him **reclaim his Sunday stall at McGolrick**; tells his full story at the table. Available for cameos; returns Book 6.
- **Pike.** Stalled, not destroyed; baffled by a thing he can't negotiate with; regroups. Recurring presence seeded.
