# Ruth

## Overview
A veteran preserve vendor at the McGolrick Park farmers market who provides Emma with the first concrete information about Hank's disappearance.

## Personality
* Warm and motherly — calls Emma "honey," expresses genuine concern for Hank
* Observant — noticed Hank was "off" during his final market day: distracted, checking his phone
* Community-minded — she's been at the market long enough to know when something isn't right, and she cares enough to say so
* Straightforward — doesn't speculate wildly, just reports what she saw and lets Emma draw her own conclusions

## Background

### The Market
Ruth runs a preserve stand at the McGolrick Park farmers market. She's been a fixture there for years — long enough to know the rhythms and the regulars. Her stand is reliable, unpretentious, and well-stocked with seasonal preserves (jams, jellies, pickled goods).

She and Hank were market neighbors — the kind of collegial relationship that develops between vendors who set up near each other week after week. Not close friends, but close enough that his sudden absence rattled her.

### What She Saw
The last time Ruth saw Hank, he seemed "off" — distracted, kept checking his phone. Then Wednesday, all the vendors got an email: Hank had given up his stall. No explanation, no notice. "Very strange for Hank. He's been here every weekend for three years. Never missed a day."

## Skills
* Preserve-making (a craft that requires patience, precision, and seasonal knowledge)
* Market community awareness — she knows who's been around, who's new, and who's missing

## Relationships
* **Emma**: A familiar face at the market. Ruth trusts her enough to share concerns about Hank openly.
* **Hank**: A fellow vendor she respected and liked. His absence genuinely worries her.
* **Dorothy**: Likely knows her from the market vendor community (both are older, established vendors).

## Character Arc
Ruth is a single-scene character in Book 1, but she serves an important narrative function: she's the first person to confirm that Hank's disappearance is unusual and potentially concerning. She represents the market community that cared about Hank — the people who noticed when he was gone.

## Secrets
* None established.
