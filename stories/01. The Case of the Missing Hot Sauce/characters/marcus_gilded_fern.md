# Marcus (The Gilded Fern)

## Overview
The stylish owner of a Williamsburg boutique who serves as one of Olivia's key neighborhood contacts and an early source of intel about Hank's strange behavior.

## Personality
* Aesthetically meticulous — his sharp haircut contrasts with his soft cashmere sweater; his shop is a curated blend of artisanal soaps, candles, and locally made pantry items
* Observant and detail-oriented — notices behavioral shifts in the vendors he works with; picks up on Hank being "jittery" before anyone else raises the alarm
* Discreet but gossipy — he lowers his voice when sharing sensitive information, creating a sense of intimacy and conspiracy, which is exactly the frequency Olivia operates on
* Loyal to the neighborhood — once the group's investigation goes public, he's fully invested; gives Olivia discounts and calls them "local heroes"
* Slightly judgmental in a benign way — describes Hank as "a little theatrical for my taste" while acknowledging his consistent sales

## Background

### The Gilded Fern
Marcus owns The Gilded Fern, a boutique on Bedford Avenue that sells artisanal soaps, hand-poured candles, and a curated selection of locally made pantry items — including, until recently, Hank's hot sauce. The shop smells of lavender and bergamot. It's the kind of Williamsburg business that exists at the intersection of gentrification and genuine community — polished enough for newcomers, authentic enough for locals.

He also carries Olivia's candles, which is how they became texting-terms friendly. Their relationship is professional but warm, the kind of neighborhood commerce that doubles as social infrastructure.

### Hank's Inventory Pull
When Hank came to pull his inventory from The Gilded Fern's shelves, Marcus noticed immediately that something was wrong. Hank paid cash to break his shelf-space contract — unusual and expensive. He seemed "jittery," kept looking at his phone, and "looked like a man who was running from something." Marcus didn't press for details, but the encounter stuck with him.

### The Investigation
Once Olivia looped him in (through their existing texting relationship), Marcus became an eager secondary source. He's the kind of person who thrives on being "in the know" — not out of malice, but because neighborhood intel is social currency in Williamsburg's small-business ecosystem. By the end of the story, he's telling customers about the group's investigation and calling them heroes.

## Skills
* Retail curation — understands local consumer trends and what sells in Williamsburg
* Neighborhood intelligence — knows the local business ecosystem intimately
* Reading people — noticed Hank's behavioral shift before it became a pattern

## Relationships
* **Olivia**: Business contact turned friendly ally. She sells candles through his shop; he shares neighborhood intel with her. Their dynamic is transactional but genuine — they're both people who thrive on information exchange.
* **Hank**: A vendor he respected professionally. Marcus didn't know Hank well personally, but the inventory pull unsettled him enough to remember the details.
* **Emma**: He knows her indirectly through Olivia and the investigation. By the end, he considers the whole group "local heroes."

## Character Arc
Marcus doesn't have a traditional arc — he's a supporting character who serves the plot by providing early intel and representing the neighborhood's investment in Hank's story. His growing enthusiasm (from cautious gossip to proudly claiming the group as heroes) mirrors the broader community response.

## Secrets
* None established. Marcus is refreshingly straightforward — what you see is what you get.

## Notes
* The Gilded Fern's aesthetic (lavender, bergamot, linen tea towels, fig jam) is a useful sensory anchor for Williamsburg's boutique culture.
* Marcus could recur in future books as a neighborhood gossip source for Olivia — a reliable first call when something seems "off" in the local business scene.
