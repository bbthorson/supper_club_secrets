# The Case of the Missing Hot Sauce — Story Outline

**Structure:** 22 chapters across 4 meals (5 + 7 + 5 + 5)
**Timeline:** ~3 weeks (Sunday to Sunday to Sunday)
**Save the Cat midpoint:** Ch12 (false victory) at ~55%

## Group Dynamics Threaded Throughout
* **Emma** and **Noah** clash on traditional vs. tech approaches (Meals 1–2).
* **Elijah** and **Oliver** pair up for analytical investigation work (Meal 2).
* **Jasper** provides the breakthrough in Meal 2, but his loose lips cause the crisis in Meal 3.
* **Olivia** gathers crucial intel through social connections (Meals 1–3).
* **Oliver** emerges as an unexpected leader under pressure (Meals 3–4).
* **Jasper's** redemption arc is finding Hank solo while the group fights the campaign (Meal 4).

---

## Meal 1 — The Setup (Chapters 1–5)

**Week 1, Sunday.**

### Opening Image + Theme Stated (Ch. 1: "The Missing Hot Sauce")
* A crisp October Sunday in Brooklyn. **Emma**, three weeks out from quitting Bistro Lavande, heads to the McGolrick Park farmers market (her usual Sunday market) to buy ingredients for dinner with friends.
* She discovers **Hank's** stall is empty — a raw gap in the row. **Dorothy** (honey vendor, neighboring stall) tells her he seemed "off" his last week, then gave up his stall overnight with no explanation.
* Dorothy gives Emma a small bottle of Hank's hot sauce — he left it with her and said his great-grandmother "would've wanted her peppers shared. Just in case."
* Emma calls Hank's number — straight to generic voicemail.
* Back home, Emma cooks. She uses the hot sauce in her butternut squash soup. The transformation is immediate — the heat blooms underneath the sweetness. The soup has found its story.

### Set-up (Ch. 2: "The First Supper")
* **Jasper** arrives first (claiming a daylight savings mix-up). **Olivia**, **Oliver**, and **Noah** arrive together. **Elijah** is running late from work.
* The group catches up over wine — we learn their personalities through natural banter, Noah's bad wine choices, and Jasper playing host.
* Emma serves the soup. The group's reaction is immediate — the sauce is extraordinary. Emma shows them the bottle and tells the story of Hank, Dorothy, and "just in case." The disappearance becomes personal because they've tasted what's at stake.

### Catalyst (Ch. 3: "A Simmering Suspicion")
* **Elijah** arrives from work. **Oliver** recaps the Hank situation. Elijah tastes the soup and confirms it's exceptional.
* **Olivia** reveals she texted **Marcus** (boutique owner at The Gilded Fern) who confirmed Hank came and took all his inventory back, acting "jittery" — "like a man running from something."
* The group's curiosity shifts from idle chat to genuine intrigue. **Olivia** produces a hat with numbers for a theory competition.

### Debate (Ch. 4: "Stirring the Pot")
* Each friend presents their theory for **Elijah** to judge:
    * **Oliver** (#1): Reluctantly suggests kidnapping by a secret business partner.
    * **Emma** (#2): "Big Hot Sauce" — corporations crushing artisanal competition. Played for laughs, but she's closest to the truth.
    * **Olivia** (#3): Hank was swept away by a wealthy socialite. Full theatrical performance.
    * **Jasper** (#4): Aliens. The CIA. The Russians. All of the above. He knocks over his wine.
    * **Noah** (#5): CIA destabilizing the global pepper market via targeted supply chain disruption.
* **Elijah** can't pick — they're all too outlandish.

### The Plan (Ch. 5: "Mise en Place")
* **Jasper** proposes they investigate their theories during the week and reconvene with a potluck.
* Dish assignments via hat draw:
    * **Olivia** & **Oliver**: Entree (volunteered — "there are two of us")
    * **Elijah**: Second course (group begs him not to make bland quinoa)
    * **Emma**: Dessert
    * **Noah**: Appetizers (swapped with Jasper)
    * **Jasper**: Wine (swapped with Noah)

## Meal 2 — Investigation (Chapters 6–12)

**Week 2, Monday–Sunday.** One character per day, investigation builds through personality-driven chain.

### Breaking into Act 2 — Investigation Chapters (Chs. 6–11)

* **Ch. 6 "A Fine Sieve" (Oliver, Monday):** Oliver thought the plan was preposterous, but he wasn't going to show up empty-handed. Spends the afternoon at the NY Public Library Rose Main Reading Room. Methodically searches the Dept. of Buildings database. Discovers an anonymous LLC (#2847) filed for a waterfront restaurant two days after Hank disappeared. Goes home to Olivia, but she's already asleep.

* **Ch. 7 "Gathering the Ingredients" (Olivia, Tuesday):** Olivia doesn't need an excuse to talk to people, but having one makes it more fun. Visits Marcus at The Gilded Fern, sources stew ingredients AND intel on the same Bedford Ave trip. Comes home to Oliver, who shares his LLC finding. She pushes him: "Tell the group." He won't. She files it away.

* **Ch. 8 "The Bottom Line" (Elijah, Wednesday):** Olivia calls Elijah about a tailor recommendation, then pivots to sharing Oliver's finding. Elijah reads the group correctly — Oliver won't share it, Noah will shrug it off without structured data. He posts the LLC to the group chat himself, framing it carefully. Keeps his own financial model private for now — he'll present it at dinner if the numbers hold up.

* **Ch. 9 "A Pinch Too Much" (Noah, Thursday):** Noah had already tried Monday evening — searched for Hank's digital footprint, found nothing, gave up. Elijah's group text re-engages him with structured data. He traces the LLC through corporate registries, subsidiary mappings, Delaware holding companies. Ends mid-investigation — there's more here than he thought.

* **Ch. 10 "Loose Lips" (Jasper, Friday):** Jasper's been watching the group chat all week and everyone's contributing except him. Competitive itch. He walks toward the High Line in Chelsea, scrolling his contacts. Calls **Brenda Marquez**, an old friend in commercial real estate. Tells her the whole story (doesn't name Emma). Brenda warns him. Jasper doesn't notice her missed calls that night. Brenda recognizes the LLC and calls the mogul's people.

* **Ch. 11 "The Empty Stall" (Emma, Saturday):** Emma visits McCarren Park Greenmarket for the first time — checking out Hank's Saturday market. His stall has been filled by a new vendor (jarred salsa, string lights, chalkboard sign). The market has moved on. She goes to **Sofia's** cheese shop nearby. Sofia tells her about Hank's "big offer to go legit," and about **Paolo** the pickle guy — same playbook, sued out of existence. A pattern emerges.

### Fun and Games + Midpoint / False Victory (Ch. 12: "Coming to a Boil")

* **Sunday evening.** Second supper club — potluck at Emma's.
* The group already knows about the LLC from the group chat. Olivia opens with "We all saw the group chat. Who's got more?"
* Each person shares what they learned beyond the shared baseline: Olivia's Marcus intel (jittery), Emma's Dorothy/Sofia findings (scared, Paolo precedent), Elijah's financial model (presented fresh — low-to-mid six figures motive), Noah's corporate trace (patents for "shelf-stable capsicum-based flavor enhancers").
* **Jasper's reveal:** Brenda confirmed the LLC belongs to the restaurant mogul buying up Williamsburg.
* **Celebration.** They've actually solved it. "Big Hot Sauce" was real.
* **Setback (hidden):** As Jasper walks home, his phone buzzes: *"Hey—I need you to call me. It's important. Please don't talk to anyone else about the LLC until we talk."* He doesn't see it until morning. By then, it's already too late.

### B Story — Emma's Secret
* **Emma** runs "Kinky Kitchen," an anonymous OnlyFans account — barefoot cooking content. Nothing explicit, but it's her most reliable income since leaving Bistro Lavande. She's terrified of exposure. Seeded subtly in Meals 1–2 (the "few other things" she's making work, the math she doesn't want to do).

## Meal 3 — Crisis (Chapters 13–17)

**Week 3, Tuesday/Wednesday–Sunday.**

### Bad Guys Close In (Ch. 13: "Service for One")
* Signs of trouble arrive before the C&D. Small moments that something is wrong — surveillance, questions being asked in the neighborhood, the sense of being watched. Individually dismissible, collectively a pattern.
* Then the hammer falls: a process server delivers a Cease & Desist to **Emma** at her apartment. Legal threats of defamation and tortious interference. Attached: a handwritten note — *"We know all your secrets. Kinky Kitchen, isn't it?"*
* Emma panics. Calls Elijah — voicemail. Calls **Olivia**, who drops everything and rallies the group for an emergency Wednesday dinner.

### All Is Lost — Part 1 (Ch. 14: "The Seamless Supper")
* Emergency dinner at Emma's. Thai food from Seamless — nobody can cook. The ordering process itself becomes stress-fueled chaos.
* **Elijah** arrives from work, reads the document, declares it "toothless" but acknowledges the intimidation is real.
* **Emma confesses** about Kinky Kitchen. Braces for judgment. Instead: Olivia is fierce ("it's your body, it's your kitchen"), Jasper is earnest ("doesn't matter"), Elijah calls it "a market inefficiency monetized with minimal overhead." The relief of being accepted.

### All Is Lost — Part 2 (Ch. 15: "A Bitter Taste")
* **Noah** asks the key question: How did they know to target Emma specifically? Information flow analysis — tracing what went where.
* **Jasper confesses.** He told Brenda everything. The room erupts. The sharpest fight the group has ever had. Noah lashes out. Jasper admits he's always felt like "the liability."
* **Oliver stands up.** In the most words he's ever spoken at once, he rallies the group. Draws on his experience moderating online communities: "The playbook is always the same: create fear, create division, wait for the target to self-destruct. The counter-strategy is also always the same."

### Break into Three (Ch. 16: "The Recipe for Revenge")
* The group formulates the "swarm" strategy: instead of going to one big outlet, distribute the story anonymously to hundreds of small ones — food bloggers, neighborhood gossip pages, local influencers.
* Roles assigned:
    * **Noah**: VPNs, anonymous accounts, digital infrastructure
    * **Olivia**: Local contacts, neighborhood tastemakers
    * **Emma**: Food industry influencers (knows who's petty enough to publish out of spite)
    * **Oliver** & **Elijah**: Fact-checking, building a clean press kit (permits, financial model, narrative)
    * **Jasper**: Find Hank. His redemption mission. He says he "knows somebody" — plays it light, plays it like Jasper. But the group feels what's underneath. He leaves.

### Preparation (Ch. 17: "Sharpening the Knives")
* **Vignettes.** Each character goes to their corner to prepare their piece of the campaign.
    * Noah tests VPN setups and builds anonymous accounts from his apartment.
    * Olivia works her phone and maps her network of local contacts and tastemakers.
    * Oliver and Elijah build the evidence package — permits, financial model, clean narrative.
    * Emma drafts the story, identifies which food industry influencers to target.
    * Jasper texts from the road — he's started making calls.
* Each vignette shows how their specific personality and skills serve the mission. Mirrors the Meal 2 investigation chapters but compressed into a montage.

## Meal 4 — Resolution (Chapters 18–22)

### Finale — Campaign Launch (Ch. 18: "Turning Up the Heat")
* The coordinated campaign launches. The group is physically separate but working in unison — the group chat is the connective tissue.
* Initial results: small Brooklyn blogs pick it up first. A food Instagram account with decent reach. A neighborhood newsletter.
* Hope builds. The story is spreading. They're functioning as a team for the first time — not theorizing around a dinner table, but executing together.
* Jasper checks in via text. He's making calls, hitting dead ends, but not giving up.

### Finale — Pushback (Ch. 19: "[Title TBD]")
* The counterattack arrives. A conservative commentator dismisses the story as anti-capitalist whining. Takedown notices hit some of the blogs. One of their anonymous accounts gets traced — close call.
* The group's confidence cracks. Someone suggests they should stop before it gets worse. The campaign might fail.
* The chapter ends on a low note — doubt, fear that they've made things worse.

### Finale — Jasper's Journey (Ch. 20: "Farm to Table")
* **Jasper's solo chapter.** The only time the book leaves Brooklyn.
* Twenty phone calls to nurseries in Maryland and Pennsylvania. A woman at a small nursery mentions the **Meadowlight Collective** — an intentional community two hours west of Philadelphia.
* Jasper drives out. Finds Hank in a greenhouse tending striped pepper plants.
* He tells Hank everything. Hank is moved — "They did all that? For me?"
* Jasper's unspoken guilt and relief. This is his redemption — not announced, not performed, just done.
* Hank agrees to come to dinner.

### Finale — Breakthrough (Ch. 21: "[Title TBD]")
* Back in Brooklyn, **Oliver** deploys his secret weapon — a meticulously sourced rebuttal document with talking points, distributed to their existing allies.
* The rebuttal spreads. A major food critic picks up the story and adopts their exact framing. Sentiment flips.
* The mogul's investors start pulling out. The C&D is formally withdrawn. The waterfront restaurant is shelved.
* The tide has turned. The group can breathe.
* Jasper texts the group chat: he found Hank. He's bringing him to dinner.

### Final Image (Ch. 22: "Family Meal")
* Celebratory dinner at Emma's. Two weeks after the emergency dinner (or sooner — TBD based on timeline).
* The food reflects resolution and joy. Each person's dish shows growth.
* The door buzzes. **Jasper** walks in with **Hank**, holding an unlabeled bottle of hot sauce.
* Hank tells his story — the lowball offer for his family's trademark, the threats when he tried to negotiate, running to Meadowlight. A pro bono lawyer has since helped him file a proper trademark.
* Emma's OnlyFans is public knowledge now; the response was overwhelmingly supportive.
* The group agrees to continue the supper club with rotating hosts. Jasper is already pitching a new mystery. The first case is solved, but the Supper Club is just getting started.

## Mystery Solution Grid

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| **What really happened?** | Restaurant mogul **Garrett Pike** used an anonymous LLC to file patents on Hank's hot sauce recipe category and pressure him out of the market. When Hank refused a lowball trademark buyout, the mogul threatened to bury him in legal fees. |
| **Who is responsible?** | Garrett Pike, operating through LLC #2847 and a larger hospitality group. |
| **Why did they do it?** | Hank's artisanal brand was a rising competitor. Pike wanted to acquire the trademark cheaply and own the heritage hot sauce category. |
| **How was it discovered?** | Oliver found the anonymous LLC filing. Noah traced it to the hospitality group and its hot sauce patents. Elijah's financial model confirmed the motive. Brenda (via Jasper) confirmed the mogul's identity. |
| **How was it resolved?** | Anonymous, distributed social media campaign exposing Pike. A major food critic amplified the story. Investor pressure forced Pike to withdraw the C&D and shelve the waterfront restaurant. Hank filed a proper trademark with pro bono legal help. |
| **Fair play check** | Yes — all clues available to reader by end of Meal 3. The LLC, the patents, the financial motive, and the mogul's identity are all established before the resolution. |
