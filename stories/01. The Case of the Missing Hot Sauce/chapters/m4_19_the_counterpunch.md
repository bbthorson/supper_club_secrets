*[PLACEHOLDER — Pushback. The campaign might fail.]*

- A conservative commentator dismisses the story as anti-capitalist whining. The framing spreads.
- Takedown notices from the mogul's lawyers hit some of the blogs that posted.
- One of their anonymous accounts gets traced — close call. The group's digital cover is thinner than they thought.
- Someone (maybe Noah, maybe Elijah) suggests they should stop before it gets worse. Real doubt.
- The group's confidence cracks. The campaign that felt like momentum in Ch18 now feels like it might collapse.
- Chapter ends on a low note — doubt, fear that they've made things worse, that the mogul has more resources than they can fight.

**Tone:** Tension, doubt. The emotional counterweight to Ch18's hope. This needs to feel real enough that the reader isn't sure they'll win.
