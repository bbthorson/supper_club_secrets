# Brenda Chen

## Overview
A sharp commercial real estate professional and old acquaintance of Jasper's who inadvertently becomes the bridge between the group's investigation and the mogul's retaliation.

## Personality
* Professional and polished — operates in a high-stakes industry where information is currency and relationships are transactional
* Genuinely fond of Jasper despite finding him exhausting — she's learned to filter his chaos for useful signal
* Pragmatic about loyalty — when the information Jasper gave her created a conflict of interest, she chose the client over the friend, a decision she regretted
* Risk-aware — she warned Jasper to be careful, which makes her betrayal more tragic than malicious
* Not a villain — she's a person caught between personal affection and professional obligation in a system that rewards the latter

## Background

### The Jasper Connection
Brenda knows Jasper from "a past life" — the exact nature of their connection is deliberately vague. Given Jasper's old-money background, they likely crossed paths through overlapping social circles: family events, private school alumni networks, or the kind of New York scene where trust fund kids and real estate professionals share cocktail parties. She's one of many people in Jasper's sprawling, improbable contact list.

They haven't spoken in years when Jasper calls her about the LLC. She picks up anyway — that's the kind of pull Jasper has, even on people who know better.

### Professional Life
Brenda works at Apex Realty, a firm that handles high-end commercial real estate in Brooklyn and Manhattan. Her client list includes the restaurant mogul whose LLC is at the center of the mystery. She recognized the LLC number immediately when Jasper gave it to her — she'd seen it in recent filings from the mogul's hospitality group.

### The Fateful Call
After Jasper told her everything — the investigation, the LLC, Emma's name as the driving force — Brenda faced a choice. She could sit on the information, or she could flag it to her client. She chose the latter, calling the mogul's people to report "a potential issue with one of your Brooklyn acquisitions."

She didn't intend for Emma to be personally targeted. She certainly didn't know about Kinky Kitchen. But she gave the mogul enough information to connect the dots and retaliate. Her follow-up text to Jasper — asking to confirm Emma's name because "my contact asked" — suggests she realized too late what she'd set in motion.

## Skills
* Commercial real estate expertise — understands LLC structures, property filings, and the players in Brooklyn's development landscape
* Professional networking — knows who owns what and who's buying what in the borough
* Reading people — she can filter Jasper's ten-minute manic monologue down to the three facts that matter

## Relationships
* **Jasper**: Old acquaintance from overlapping social circles. She finds him entertaining but wouldn't call him a close friend. His call put her in an impossible position.
* **The Mogul**: A client. Their relationship is professional and transactional — she processes his firm's filings and deals. She knows he's ruthless, which is why she warned Jasper.
* **The Group**: She never meets the rest of them. To the group, she exists only as the person Jasper called, the link in the chain that exposed Emma.

## Character Arc
Brenda serves a specific structural purpose in the story: she's the mechanism by which Jasper's good intentions turn into real consequences. She's not evil — she's a professional who made a professional choice that had deeply personal fallout for people she didn't know. Her story is a quiet illustration of how corporate systems can weaponize ordinary people's ordinary decisions.

## Secrets
* She likely feels genuine guilt about what happened. Her warning text to Jasper ("Just want to make sure I gave him the right info") reads as someone already regretting the call she made.
* The exact nature of her professional obligation — whether she *had* to report or *chose* to — is ambiguous. That ambiguity is the point.

## Notes
* Brenda never appears on-page — she exists entirely through Jasper's phone call (Ch. 9) and her text message (Ch. 11).
* Her last name is Chen (established in Ch. 9: "Brenda Chen from Apex Realty").
* She's a useful character to bring back in future books if the group ever needs a reluctant real estate contact — someone who owes them one.
