### Emma's Apartment
**Address:** Williamsburg, near McGolrick Park
**Type:** Railroad apartment, small but character-filled
**Details:**
- Cramped but beloved kitchen, the heart of the space
- Dining table that seats six with effort
- Eclectic décor, some inherited from grandmother
- Fire escape with a view of limited usefulness
- Furnished with hand-me-downs and street finds
- Intercom buzzer is harsh and electric
- Wine glasses, corkscrew (two drawers to the left — Jasper always forgets), and milk crate seating supplement the limited furniture
- Folding chairs and a loveseat round out the living room

**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 1

### Olivia & Oliver's Place
**Address:** Clinton Hill (moved in after wedding)
**Type:** One-bedroom apartment
**Details:**
- Olivia decorated; Oliver keeps it organized
- Their dynamic reflected in the space: warm personality meets systematic order
- Oliver handles bills and maintenance schedules; Olivia handles the social calendar

**First Appearance:** Book 1, Chapter 7 (briefly — Oliver shows Olivia his findings)

## Recurring Brooklyn Locations

### McGolrick Park Farmers Market (Down to Earth)
**Type:** Weekly farmers market
**Schedule:** Sunday mornings, year-round (9am–2pm)
**Location:** Center of McGolrick Park between Driggs and Nassau Ave off Russell St, Greenpoint
**Google Maps:** [McGolrick Park](https://maps.app.goo.gl/PmtQrQu95p6Wnnm89) | [Farmers Market](https://maps.app.goo.gl/vboESMC7b2UncNW67)
**Details:**
- Smaller, community-feel market run by Down to Earth Markets — local farmers and area food/craft makers
- Stalls overflow with autumn bounty: glossy squash, root vegetables with dirt, herb bundles tied with twine
- Air smells like apples, fresh bread, and something smoky from the grill vendor
- Hank's hot sauce stall was here for three years — his Sunday market (bright red tablecloth, hand-painted signs, bottles in precise pyramids)
- Dorothy's honey stand (neighboring Hank's stall) — Dorothy gives Emma Hank's last bottle in Ch1
- Emma's "home" market — she lives walking distance from here

**Story Significance:** Where Emma discovers Hank is missing and gets the bottle from Dorothy (Book 1, Ch. 1). Hank's stall remains empty here.

### McCarren Park Greenmarket
**Type:** Weekly farmers market
**Schedule:** Saturday mornings
**Location:** McCarren Park, Williamsburg
**Details:**
- Larger and more crowded than McGolrick — the bigger Saturday market
- Hank's hot sauce stall was here too — his Saturday market
- After Hank's disappearance, his stall is quickly filled by a new vendor (jarred salsa)
- Near Sofia's cheese shop

**Story Significance:** Emma visits for the first time in Ch. 11 (Meal 2), checking out Hank's Saturday market. Finds his stall already replaced — the market has moved on. Contrast with McGolrick, where his spot is still empty.

### The Gilded Fern
**Type:** Boutique on Bedford Avenue
**Owner:** Marcus
**Details:**
- Sells artisanal soaps, hand-poured candles, and curated locally made pantry items
- Air smells of lavender, bergamot, and cedar
- Carries Olivia's candles and (formerly) Hank's hot sauce
- Linen tea towels, glass jars of fig jam — polished Williamsburg aesthetic
- Marcus and Olivia are on texting terms through their business relationship

**Story Significance:** Olivia gathers key intel from Marcus here (Book 1, Ch. 8). Marcus becomes a recurring neighborhood ally.

### The Coffee Hole (Fictional)
**Type:** Local coffee shop
**Details:**
- Where Olivia gathers neighborhood gossip
- Regulars know each other by name
- Where Olivia reconnected with Elijah ~3 years before Book 1 — spotted him, walked over, talked for 45 minutes
- TBD: owner/barista character for future books

**Story Significance:** Referenced but not a primary scene location in Book 1. Potential future gathering spot.

### Sofia's Cheese Shop
**Type:** Neighborhood cheese shop
**Owner:** Sofia
**Details:**
- A Williamsburg fixture for twenty years
- Sofia wraps wedges of aged cheddar in wax paper with practiced hands, apron dusted with flour
- Wheels of parmesan, specialty items
- Emma is a regular customer

**Story Significance:** Where Emma learns Hank got "a big offer to go legit" (Book 1, Ch. 7/8)

## Canon Facts to Maintain

- Emma lives between the Graham Ave L and Nassau Ave G stops (Williamsburg/Greenpoint border), walking distance from McGolrick Park
- Noah lives on the Williamsburg waterfront
- Olivia & Oliver live in Clinton Hill (moved in after wedding, "when the neighborhood was still more promise than proof")
- Jasper lives in Chelsea, off 8th Ave (quiet rebellion from UES family; has access to family money)
- Elijah lives in Bed-Stuy near Classon Ave G stop
- Hank sold at two markets: McGolrick Park (Sunday) and McCarren Park (Saturday)
- Dorothy (honey vendor) sells at McGolrick, next to Hank's stall
- At McGolrick, Hank's stall remains empty after his disappearance
- At McCarren, Hank's stall is filled by a new vendor (jarred salsa) by the time Emma visits in Ch11
- The supper club has been meeting regularly before Book 1 begins
- Williamsburg is portrayed as gentrified but still maintains pockets of local community
- The Coffee Hole is where Olivia reconnected with Elijah
- The Gilded Fern is on Bedford Avenue
