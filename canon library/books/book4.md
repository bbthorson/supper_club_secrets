# Book 4: [Working Title TBD]

**Focal Character:** [Olivia](../characters/olivia.md)
**Month:** January
**Host:** Rotating — each meal at a different home (Olivia's "new year, new energy" idea)
- Meal 1: Emma's (familiar ground)
- Meal 2: Noah's (minimalist discomfort, comedy)
- Meal 3: Jasper's (chaotic warmth, vinyl on the turntable)
- Meal 4: Olivia & Oliver's (coming home, resolution)

**Mystery Concept:**
One of Olivia's many past business contacts — a woman she "collected" during her pop-up vintage sales phase — is accused of running an elaborate art fraud scheme through a small gallery in Carroll Gardens. Fake provenance documents, forged authentication letters, stolen pieces laundered through estate sales. The accusation comes from a rival gallerist, and the evidence looks damning.

But Olivia doesn't believe it. She knew this woman. She liked this woman. And the accusation doesn't match the person she remembers — or does it? The investigation forces Olivia to confront the difference between knowing everyone and truly knowing anyone.

**Why Olivia:**
- The mystery lives in her world — networking, local business, curated taste
- Her superpower (reading people, collecting contacts) becomes both asset and liability
- January's post-holiday emptiness amplifies her restlessness — she's between projects again, and the investigation fills the void
- Her relationship with Oliver gets complicated when Oliver operates confidently and independently during the investigation (building on his Book 2 growth), and she doesn't know whether to be proud or threatened

**Character Arc:**
Olivia has always been the one who connects people, who sees the best in everyone, who collects relationships like curios. This mystery forces her to reckon with the cost of surface-level connection. Did she actually know this woman, or did she just like the idea of knowing her? The investigation also confronts her with a mirror: the accused woman is, in many ways, another version of Olivia — charming, networked, always moving to the next thing. The difference is one of them got caught.

**Secret Progression:** Her anxiety about Oliver's dependence surfaces when, for the first time, Oliver doesn't need her to translate or advocate for him. He's comfortable, he's contributing independently, and Olivia feels — unexpectedly — lost. She doesn't voice it, but Emma notices something is off.

**Rotating Homes — World-Building Opportunities:**
- **Meal 1 at Emma's:** Comfort, familiarity. The group is back where it started. January cold outside, warm kitchen inside.
- **Meal 2 at Noah's:** Comedy of minimalism. Not enough chairs. No wine glasses (he uses geometric tumblers). Air purifier too loud. But the East River view at night is stunning, and Noah is surprisingly proud of his space.
- **Meal 3 at Jasper's:** The Chelsea apartment is a revelation. Books everywhere, vinyl organized obsessively (contradicting the chaos), mismatched but comfortable. Jasper is a natural, relaxed host — the opposite of what everyone expected. Seeds of "who is this guy really?" planted for Book 6.
- **Meal 4 at Olivia & Oliver's:** Their space tells their story — her warmth, his order. The resolution dinner here feels like Olivia reclaiming her home and her sense of self.

**January Texture:** Post-holiday quiet. The neighborhood feels smaller in winter — fewer people on the streets, shops closing early, the G train even less reliable than usual. Carroll Gardens in January: brownstone stoops dusted with salt, café windows fogged from the inside. The art gallery world as a pocket of warmth and pretension against the cold.

**Minor Character Opportunities:**
- New: The accused gallerist (Olivia's former contact) — sympathetic, complicated
- New: The rival gallerist who made the accusation — not a simple villain
- Marcus surfaces (The Gilded Fern carries some of the same artists)
- New: An estate sale regular who notices patterns in what's being sold

**Reputation Status:** The accused woman reaches out to Olivia specifically because she's "heard about what you and your friends did for that hot sauce guy and the credit union thing." This is the first time the group's track record is explicitly cited by a stranger. Olivia is flattered. Noah is uncomfortable. Jasper is delighted.
