I.

What the research changed

Three currents in 2026 restaurant menu design, and what each one settled for us.

The bistro revival is real

Restaurant identity is in a full mid-century swing: chunky, exaggerated serifs pulled from diner menus and roadside signage, modernized rather than pastiched. The bold header you loved in the Sunday Circular isn't a detour from menu design — it's the current center of it.

Old meets new, in pairs

The strongest identities pair one characterful, decorative serif with one quiet workhorse face — Albertus with Maison Neue, a wonky display with a plain sans. Our pairing follows the pattern: Fraunces does the personality, Literata does the reading, Archivo does the chores.

One pop, not three

Palette direction is earthy neutrals with a single bold accent. v1 had two reds competing (bordeaux and tomato). v2 keeps one: Hot Sauce — which, given Book 1, is the only accent this brand could honestly have.

86'd from v1

  • Three directions — one direction now: The Menu. After Hours survives as the night reading theme; the Circular survives as sticker ephemera.
  • Didot elegance — the wordmark trades fine-dining engraving for bistro-revival weight. Same menu structure, more appetite.
  • Bordeaux as lead accent — replaced by Hot Sauce red. One pop, and it's the one the first book is named after.

Why Fraunces earns the job

Fraunces is a free, open-source revival of the "wonky" turn-of-the-century faces — Windsor chief among them — that the entire bistro revival is built on. At Black it's the chunky poster voice you loved; at a light italic it's menu romance. One font file covers the whole register, which keeps the site fast and the brand impossible to assemble wrong.

Reading is sacred

Fraunces never sets a paragraph. Chapters are read in Literata — a serif designed for long-form screen reading — at a 65–70 character measure. The rule of thumb: Fraunces is the front of house, Literata is the meal itself.

After hours — the night service

Dark mode isn't inverted linen; it's the same restaurant after the candles are lit. Bottle green ground, candlelight accents, cream text. Same typefaces, same menu structure — the brand gains a day/night duality that mirrors cozy/mystery.
