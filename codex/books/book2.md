# Book 2: [Working Title TBD]

**Focal Character:** [Oliver](../characters/oliver.md)
**Month:** November
**Host:** Olivia & Oliver (their Williamsburg one-bedroom)

**Mystery Concept:**
A series of small, beloved businesses along the G line corridor are being forced out — not through obvious gentrification, but through a quiet, bureaucratic squeeze. Permits delayed indefinitely. Landmark protections mysteriously withdrawn. Building inspections that always find just enough violations to shut a place down for weeks.

Oliver notices the pattern first — not through the supper club, but through his secret online life. His pseudonymous zoning-violations Twitter account has been tracking anomalies for weeks. One of his subreddit contacts flags a connection: all the targeted businesses sit on parcels that were recently rezoned under a little-noticed amendment. Someone is gaming the city's administrative machinery to clear blocks for development without ever having to buy anyone out.

The mystery stumbles into the group's lap when Sofia (from Book 1) mentions that her cheese shop just failed an inspection for the first time in twenty years — for a code violation she's never heard of.

**Why Oliver:**
- The mystery lives in his professional wheelhouse (zoning, permits, municipal records)
- His secret online life becomes essential to the investigation — and threatens to be exposed
- His relationship with Olivia gets tested when the investigation requires him to operate independently, publicly, and confidently
- The canon note about "an awkward encounter with Jasper at a city office" pays off here

**Character Arc:**
Oliver must step out from behind his screens — both the anonymous ones and the ones Olivia provides. When his pseudonymous posts get dangerously close to identifying the scheme, he faces a choice: protect his anonymous comfort zone or go public with what he knows. The group sees the confident, articulate person his 3,000 Twitter followers already know.

**Secret Progression:** Oliver's online life edges toward exposure. By the end, at least one person in the group (probably Jasper, who stumbles across Oliver's account during the city office encounter) knows or strongly suspects. Oliver doesn't fully confess, but the door is cracked open.

**Thanksgiving Beat:** The November setting puts Thanksgiving in play. Meal 3 or 4 could fall on or near the holiday — forcing the group to balance investigation with family obligations. Oliver's quiet New Jersey parents could visit, giving us a window into who he was before Olivia. Elijah's family obligations pull him briefly off-screen (seeding Book 3).

**Seasonal Texture:** Late autumn. Bare trees, early dark, the particular cold that comes off the East River. First real coat weather. Thanksgiving grocery chaos at the market. The neighborhood shifting from fall charm to winter endurance.

**Minor Character Opportunities:**
- Sofia returns (her shop is threatened)
- Marcus at The Gilded Fern may also be affected (different block, same pattern)
- New: A city clerk or inspector who's been pressured to rubber-stamp violations
- New: A longtime tenant organizer or community board member who's been fighting this quietly

**Reputation Status:** Still feels accidental. "We helped Hank, and now Sofia needs help too." But someone outside the group — maybe the tenant organizer — says something like "I heard you all handled that hot sauce situation."
